Design Principles

Take the initiative. Sketch more ideas. Work on the problem, not the solution. Help everyone succeed.

When I began my career, these were the cornerstones of how I approached my work. I enjoy understanding how systemic problems originate, and breaking those pieces down to their most basic solvable state. Whether it’s through UX, service design, or illustration – I hope to always apply these principles to my work.

Take the initiative.

As I developed my career, I have always felt an urge to act. Whether large or small, any work that iteratively improves a problem is worthwhile. I prefer to lean into problems head first, breaking the incomprehensible into manageable improvements.

Sketch more ideas.

While there is limited effectiveness in user testing, the iterative design process is crucial. I believe in sketching as many ideas as possible in lo-fidelity to collaborate and get to the best design possible.

Work on the problem, not the solution.

While meeting new problems, it's easy to focus on providing a series of solutions. I thoroughly enjoy user-centered problems, and uncovering user points of friction.

Help everyone succeed.

There is no "I" in team. I enjoy collaborative sessions, and creating a shared understanding for complex problems. I prefer to bring various teams together, and help us all feel heard and validated.
