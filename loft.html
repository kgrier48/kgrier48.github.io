Loft Design System

As a design lead for a design system, my role involves maintaining and improving the system to ensure that it is effective and efficient for designers and developers. This includes defining design standards, creating and updating design assets and components, and collaborating with cross-functional teams to ensure that the design system is consistently applied across the organization.

Responsibilities Included: Design Lead, Product Management, Art Direction

Establish the Foundations

Creating an Effective Design System

The start of my journey into Design Systems began shortly after I started working at Xometry. The team was working to build an official design system that gathered the disparate components across multiple platforms, and creating a single space of typescript enabled components and tokens.

sync_problem
Understanding the Problem

At the start of my experience with the design system, I began learning the in's and out's of our existing system in Sketch. The components were scaterred into multiple locations and versions, without a single source of truth or documentation.

cell_merge
Creating a Single Source of Truth

While the development team was hard at work, setting versioning, building components, and writing out technical documentation. I began to establish a single "UI Kit" in Abstract, after conversations with the other designers, we built up a set of best practices and corrected our reference files. Going from multitudes of disparate libraries, and slowly transitioning to a single version controlled library file.

repeat
Versioning and Improvements

As the design system began to be rolled out to the rest of the organization, my team established a short weekly recurring meeting for users of the design system to discuss bugs, ideas, and opportunities to build things together.

Create a Vision

Investment Strategy and Vision

While ensuring that the design system was maintained, and that weekly conversations continued. The senior engineering lead and myself worked with, and reported to our executive sponsors to build an investment strategy and vision for further large updates to the design system.

flag
Establishing OKRs

Before we could establish key tactical improvements, the senior engineer and myself worked to identify key objectives we wanted to impact for the design system and it's usage. Our key objectives included: creating a unified system that newly acquired companies could adopt easily, establishing animation standards, improving rendering tests, and developing subject matter experts across the company.

groups
Building the Team

Once we dicussed and finalized our strategy with our executive sponsors, we creating a call-for-volunteers to be regularly contributing to the design system objectives. This group was intended to be a wide variety of engineers and designers from all aspects of the organization. The goal being that these engineers and deisgners could then share knowledge with the rest of their native teams.

signpost
Product Ownership

After the team was established, we began creating pairs of designers and developers to tackle new components, bugs, and documentation updates. We established a kanban system that allowed individuals to pull tickets and complete work as they were available, all driving to our key objectives.

Iterate and Improve

Maintaining a Design System

After the launching of a design system, one of the most challenging aspects is keeping momentum and discussions around improving and maintaing the system. As a collaborative pair, myself and a senior technical engineer took it upon ourselves to spearhead this continued effort in a series of ways.

grid_guides
Establishing Usage Standards

As part of the strategy to align the company behind a design system, I helped to author Technical Standards for version usage of the design system. This was supported and approved by the Chief Technical Officer, and led to key discussions of how to best technically use the design system across the many parts of the organization.

speaker_notes
Facilitation and Decision Making

Another key aspect of keeping the system to evolve was my regularly scheduled conversations with design and technology to identify accessiblity issues and bugs. Creating a space for design and development to collaborate to improve our system.

communication
Open Channels of Communication

Once usage standards were in place, we fostered a space in our slack to devote to not only technical but also design comments, questions, and collaborative explorations building and improving our components.
